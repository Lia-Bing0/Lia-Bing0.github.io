About

At M&T Bank I worked inside a PCI-DSS regulated environment where security was not a separate concern but part of every deployment decision. I owned CI/CD pipeline releases through multi-tier change approval, containerized legacy workloads, and enforced RBAC and least-privilege access across internal services in collaboration with InfoSec. That experience gave me something most security engineers do not have: I know what it looks like from the other side of the code review. I know why engineers cut corners under delivery pressure, how misconfigurations slip through, and what actually breaks when you try to fix them. That context is what I bring to every security engagement.

How I think

The question isn't "does this work?" It's "what risk does this introduce?" Misconfiguration is the vulnerability. Most cloud security failures aren't exotic exploits; they're defaults left unchallenged and access left unscoped.

This shapes how I approach every engagement, from OIDC federation in CI/CD pipelines, to Terraform IaC security gates, to Kubernetes workload identity. Every infrastructure decision is a security decision.
